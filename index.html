Hello

My name is Andrés Aradillas Fernández, and I am a junior at Cornell University double-majoring in Mathematics and Economics. Above all else, my true passion lies in economics, having taken graduate economic coursework both in microeconomics and econometrics, as well as served as a research assistant under various professors at Cornell University, Columbia Business School, and Harvard Business School.

On campus, I am involved with Cornell's premier undergraduate economic research journal, the Cornell Undergraduate Economic Review (official website), having served as the Editor-in-Chief for the 2022-2023 academic year. Aside from research, I am also involved with the jazz community on campus. I am an avid jazz piano player, and have performed not only in academic concerts, but also manage and lead the jam sessions on campus, as well as having performed for various official university events. On top of this, I also help manage the official website for the International Student Union (official website).

Love for Learning Languages (Linguaphilia)

Outside of all my academic and extracurricular interests, I have a strong passion for learning new languages. The number of languages I have ever studied to any extent is very long, but a list of notable entries is:

    > Spanish (native)

    > German (Deutsch)

    > Armenian (Հայերեն)

    > Swedish (Svenska)

    > Portuguese (Português)

    > Latin

    > Arabic (العربية)

    > Ancient Greek (Ελληνικά)

    > Old Norse (Norrœnt mál)

In case it was not obvious, my language interest is all over the place. The language I am currently dedicating the most amount of time to is Armenian (specifically, Eastern Armenian). I think it is an incredibly beautiful language that is slept on by a lot of polyglots, and also serves a unique challenge in that its closest relative to any other living language is Proto-Indo-European.
